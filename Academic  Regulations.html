Academic Organisational Structure

The academic structure ensures clear responsibilities at all levels.

Dean of Academic Affairs
Oversees all academic functions
Heads of Department (HoDs)
Manage staffing and HR
Programme Leaders
Ensure academic health of programmes
Semester Guides
Support student cohorts
Module Tutors
Teach and assess modules

Decision-Making Bodies

The College has key academic decision-making bodies to ensure quality and fairness.

College Academic Committee (CAC)
Oversees academic standards & policies
Programme Committee
Monitors academic health, regular programme activities
Programme Board of Examiners
Oversees assessment standards, fairness, consistency, and outcomes.

Registration and Module Enrolment

Semester Registration

All students must register at the start of each semester based on their academic progress and programme requirements.Non-registered student will not be assessed for progresssion

Module Load Limits

Students take 5 modules per semester, with a maximum of 7 modules.

Module Changes

Changes or withdrawals are allowed only within the first two weeks of the semester, with written approval.

Repeat Module

Students repeating failed modules must register at the ebgining and make full payment for each repeat at the time of registration.

Self-Funding Students

Students on self-funding must pay tuition fees before their registration is considered complete.

Assessment Regulations

Types of Assessment

Modules use a mix of continuous assessment (assignments, tests, presentations, practicals) and final exams. Assessment design aligns with learning outcomes.

Weighting & Pass Marks

Assessment weights and criteria are shared at the start of the semester. The usual pass mark is 50%, and students may be required to pass each component separately.

Deadlines & Extensions

All continuous assessments must be submitted on time. Extensions may be granted only for valid reasons. Late submissions may be penalized or rejected.

Reassessment

Reassessment is allowed if for failing up to two modules in SEE. Only the failed component is reassessed, and total marks are capped at 50%.

Examination Rules

Exam Conduct

Arrive 15 minutes early. No communication, mobile phones, or unauthorized materials in the exam hall.

Late Arrival & Absence

Entry not allowed after 30 minutes. Absence requires valid medical/documentary evidence and inform Porgramme Leader within 24 hours after missing the exam in wrting.

Cheating & Misconduct

Any breach of exam rules may result in annulment of all exams in the semester.

Deferred Exams

Permitted only for illness or serious circumstances with official documentation and inform Programme Leader in writing.

Recheck and Re-evaluation

Right to Appeal

Students may appeal Programme Board decisions within 14 days of result declaration.

Recheck of Scripts

Ensures all answers marked and counted; Nu. 200 per module (refundable if error found).

Re-evaluation of Scripts

Done by second marker; average of first and second marking is final. Fee Nu. 500 per module (non-refundable).

Where to Apply

Applications must be submitted to the Programme Leader in writing.
